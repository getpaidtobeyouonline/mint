Minted Elimination Diet Planner

Plan a structured elimination-and-reintroduction process to identify trigger foods.

Phase 1 — Eliminate (2-4 weeks)

Remove the suspect foods completely and consistently.

Phase 2 — Observe

Track how you feel. Symptoms often ease if a trigger was removed.

Phase 3 — Reintroduce (one at a time)

Add one food back for 2-3 days, watch for reactions, then test the next.

Important: Elimination diets can affect nutrition and are best done with a doctor or registered dietitian — especially for children, or anyone with a health condition. This is a planning tool, not medical advice.
